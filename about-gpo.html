FORGERY / EMBEZZLEMENT / FRAUD IN SPECIAL SAVING ACCOUNTS AT KARACHI GPO AMOUNTING TO BILLION RUPEES

The FIA conducted investigation on a reference received from PPOD Lahore regarding the subject matter. Investigations of Special Saving Bank Accounts relating to Karachi GPO.. The initial results of investigations are alarming as the fraudulent withdrawals, forgery and record tempering for withdrawal / deposit vouchers in Special Saving Accounts resulting into embezzlement are at a large scale.


The memo of returns, daily hand book and ledgers of accounts were scrutinized and detected transactions where public money is swindled by various modes of fraudulent activity i.e. insertion of digits, fake withdrawals, fake deposits.

The involved officials of GPO adopted following modus operandi for embezzlement:-

  1. The involved officials of GPO adopted following modus operandi for embezzlement.
  2. The amounts tendered for deposit by the account holder were pocketed by the concerned GPO officials simply recording the entries in the passbooks and SB-Ledgers but was not accounted for in Post Office Accounts.
  3. The GPO respective officials also found involved in bogus withdrawals by making the forged signatures of depositors / account holders in their absence.
  4. They also found involved in Parallel Banking by maintaining accounts on different ledgers or pages of same ledger.
  5. Misappropriation of amounts of profits by forging the signatures of depositors on withdrawal forms (SB-7).
  6. Fraudulent and fake withdrawal from the close accounts. The passbooks of such close accounts are required to be submitted to DAPPO Lahore which was deliberately kept in GPO for making fraudulent transactions later on by misusing the said passbooks.
  7. Fraudulent entries in SBP Memos by showing account closed without any voucher. No passbook has been submitted to DAPPO Lahore as required under rule whereas the account has been found active as per ledger.
  8. Raising the balance in the ledgers against an account without any deposit in the book of accounts and subsequently withdrawal from that account.
